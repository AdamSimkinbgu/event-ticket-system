Views tour: workspace & platform admin

17 views — the 11 owner-workspace views in views/company/ + the 6 in views/admin/ (5 platform-admin, 1 misplaced). Every one of them carries a @RequireCapability annotation. This page is also where you find the codebase's two most behaviour-rich views: the policy tree editor and the master/detail inbox.

§ 1The folder map

PackageLayoutCapability gate(s)
views/company/WorkspaceLayout (the workspace shell)Per-view, ranging from OWNER_WORKSPACE to DISSOLVE_COMPANY
views/admin/PlatformAdminLayout (orange platform shell) — except CompanySalesView which actually uses WorkspaceLayoutPer-view, in the ADMIN_BUNDLE — except again CompanySalesView which is owner-side (VIEW_COMPANY_SALES)
CompanySalesView is in the wrong folder. It's sales for the current company — an owner workspace view that belongs under views/company/. It ended up in views/admin/ for historical reasons during the V2 split and nobody's moved it yet. The route owner/sales, layout WorkspaceLayout, and capability VIEW_COMPANY_SALES all point to "owner shell." On the next refactor pass, move the file. Imports update; behaviour doesn't change.

§ 2companyWorkspace home & company list

OwnerDashboardView — route: owner · WorkspaceLayoutOWNER_WORKSPACE

The workspace home. Title + subtitle read the current company name from MockSession.currentCompany(), four KPI stats across the top, a tile grid filtered by capability at the bottom — the dashboard tile section from §12 of 03 is here.

What gets shown to whom

TileGated by
My EventsVIEW_COMPANY_EVENTS
Member InquiriesRESPOND_INQUIRIES
Sales HistoryVIEW_COMPANY_SALES
ManagersAPPOINT_MANAGER
Appoint Co-ownerAPPOINT_CO_OWNER
Purchase PoliciesEDIT_PURCHASE_POLICIES
Register New CompanyREGISTER_COMPANY (any signed-in member)

A manager with only the two default grants sees 2 tiles + the "Register" one. A founder sees all 7. The pattern is reused for every "list of clickable affordances" surface in the app.

MyCompaniesView — route: my-companies · WorkspaceLayout · @PermitAll (no cap gate)

The list of every company the user has a role in. Empty-state CTA card when the list is empty (driving signed-in members to CompanyRegistrationView); populated grid otherwise.

Why no capability gate

A member with zero companies still needs to see the empty state and the "Register your first company" CTA. Gating this view by OWNER_WORKSPACE would forward them directly to CompanyRegistrationView, skipping the explanation. The empty state IS the explanation.

CompanyRegistrationView — route: register-company · MainLayout · @PermitAll

The "become an owner" form. Sits in MainLayout (not the workspace shell) because a non-owner reaching this view shouldn't be visually inside the workspace yet. Real Vaadin TextField + EmailField + TextArea. Submit calls MockCompanies.add(…) with role "Founder", calls MockSession.setCurrentCompany(id), navigates to OwnerDashboardView.

§ 3companyEvents & the policy editor

CompanyEventListView — route: owner/events · WorkspaceLayoutVIEW_COMPANY_EVENTS

Filter chips (Status, Date range, Venue) + an LkGrid of events with per-row icon actions (Edit / Map / Policies / Sales / Cancel). The row-actions row is itself a small LkRow of LkIconBtns — the kit's simplest composition unit.

EventManagementView — route: owner/events/:eventId · WorkspaceLayoutEDIT_COMPANY_EVENTS

The event editor. ow-edit-split two-column layout: form on the left (Title, Category, Start/End, Venue, City, Max attendance, Description), three side cards on the right (Linked editors, Status, Danger zone).

The danger zone

Owner-only because of capability gating — even though the view is reachable by any user with EDIT_COMPANY_EVENTS, the "Cancel this event" card is rendered conditionally: 

col.add(buildLinkedEditorsCard(), buildStatusCard());
if (Capabilities.has(Capability.CANCEL_EVENT)) {
    col.add(buildDangerCard());
}

This is per-section gating — the canonical demonstration in 03 §13's cookbook.

PurchasePolicyEditorView — route: owner/policies · WorkspaceLayoutEDIT_PURCHASE_POLICIES

The codebase's most behavior-rich screen. Covered in detail in 02 §11 (PolicyTreeMap). Key points:

  • Java owns the model (sealed Node hierarchy: Composite + Rule); JS owns the rendering (D3.js SVG)
  • Pan/zoom via d3-zoom
  • Inline + R / + G pills next to each composite
  • Click body of node → edit dialog (rule editor or operator/delete picker)
  • Plain-English banner at the top recursively translates the current tree
  • Three operators: AND (amber), OR (amber), NOT (rose)

The other 16 views are pure Vaadin Flow. This one is the proof that the Java↔JS bridge pattern (@JsModule + Element.executeJs + @ClientCallable) works when you need it.

§ 4companyManagers & co-owners

ManagerListView — route: owner/managers · WorkspaceLayoutAPPOINT_MANAGER

Two sections: "Active managers" (grid + per-row Edit/Revoke icon buttons) and "Pending invitations" (grid only). The row actions are gated by their own capabilities: 

if (Capabilities.has(Capability.EDIT_MANAGER_PERMISSIONS)) actions.add(editBtn);
if (Capabilities.has(Capability.REVOKE_MANAGER))            actions.add(revokeBtn);

A user who has APPOINT_MANAGER (reaches the view) but lacks EDIT_MANAGER_PERMISSIONS still sees the list — just without the edit icon.

ManagerInvitationView — route: owner/managers/invite · WorkspaceLayoutAPPOINT_MANAGER

"Invite a manager" form. form-narrow single-column with the invitee field on top and a stack of LkCheckRows for permissions (one per grantable capability). Submit toasts and navigates back to the list.

OwnerAppointmentView — route: owner/owners/appoint · WorkspaceLayoutAPPOINT_CO_OWNER

The co-owner invitation form. Invitee field + read-only scope ("Live Nation Israel") + an LkBanner warning about cycle-prevention semantics. Co-owner invitations need acceptance from the invitee (II.4.8.2 #124), so the actual workflow involves MyInvitationsView on the other side.

§ 5companyInquiries & sales history

CompanyInquiryInboxView — route: owner/inquiries · WorkspaceLayoutRESPOND_INQUIRIES

The master/detail inbox. Filter chips at the top, then md-split grid below. Left card: list of MdConvRows. Right card: MdThread + reply bar.

The shared shape with SupportInboxView

If you read SupportInboxView (07 §5), this view is 90% the same code — just with company-side filter labels and different mock data. The messaging components were designed for both perspectives to share.

CompanySalesView — route: owner/sales · WorkspaceLayout · in views/admin/ (misplaced)VIEW_COMPANY_SALES

Per-company sales history. Filter chips (Date range, Event) + a ow-stats KPI row + an LkGrid of orders. Sales rows are immutable per II.4.5.2 #118 — no edit actions on the grid, just view.

§ 6companyVenue map editor

VenueMapEditorView — route: owner/venue/:eventId · WorkspaceLayoutMANAGE_VENUE_MAPS

The venue editor. ow-venue-split two-column: zones list + map preview on the left, zone editor on the right.

What the left column has

  • Zones grid (dense) listing every zone with type + capacity
  • Map preview using VkVenueMap — same component that drives buyer-side SeatPickerView, here in non-clickable preview mode
  • "+ Standing zone" / "+ Seated zone" buttons

What the right column has

  • Form for the selected zone: name + price TextFields in a 2-col grid
  • LkRadio for Seated vs Standing
  • Seated branch: two LkSteppers (Rows / Seats per row) + a VkSeatBlock live preview
  • Standing branch: LkStepper for capacity

§ 7adminPlatform admin

The five views inside the orange platform shell. Reached after signing in with an admin username (covered in 03 §11) or by toggling Admin in the dev panel.

AdminDashboardView — route: admin · PlatformAdminLayoutADMIN_WORKSPACE

Platform-level KPIs (Active companies, Live events, Open complaints, Platform revenue) + a 4-tile grid linking to Global history / Org tree / Announcements / Complaint queue. Same pattern as OwnerDashboardView but at the platform tier.

GlobalHistoryView — route: admin/global-history · PlatformAdminLayoutVIEW_GLOBAL_HISTORY

Every order across every company. Filter chips (Date range, Company, Event, Status) + KPI stats + an LkGrid of cross-company orders with status badges (Paid / Refunded / Disputed).

OrganizationalTreeView — route: admin/org-tree · PlatformAdminLayoutVIEW_ORG_TREES

Per-company appointment hierarchy — founder → owners → managers, drawn as an HTML .org-chart tree (same connector-line CSS as the OrganizationalTreeView from the V2 spec). Each node is a small card with the user's avatar + name + role badge + audit line ("Appointed by X on date").

HTML tree vs. D3 tree

Yes — we have two tree renderers now. The policy editor moved to D3 because it needed pan/zoom and interactive editing. This view uses the static HTML .org-chart CSS because the org tree is read-only and tends to be narrow (manager rosters rarely have more than 4 nodes wide). For a write-mode org editor (admin re-parenting a manager), this would need to move to D3 too.

AdminAnnouncementsView — route: admin/announcements · PlatformAdminLayoutBROADCAST_ANNOUNCEMENT

Two halves. Composer at the top: subject TextField + body TextArea + audience LkSelect (All members / Owners only / Managers only / Specific role) + Save draft / Send buttons. History grid below: past announcements with sent timestamp, audience, recipient count.

AdminComplaintQueueView — route: admin/complaints · PlatformAdminLayoutMANAGE_COMPLAINTS

The platform-admin's complaint inbox. Filter chips (Status, Company) + the md-split master/detail grid using MdConvRow + MdThread + MdReplyBar. Third application of the same messaging components — two on the member/company side (SupportInboxView, CompanyInquiryInboxView), one here.

§ 8Workspace patterns to copy

Pattern 1 — Capability-filtered tile grid

Used by OwnerDashboardView and AdminDashboardView. List candidate tiles, filter on Capabilities.has(…), render survivors. Manager with two grants sees two tiles, founder sees seven. Same UX rhythm at both tiers.

Pattern 2 — Form-narrow + section cards

Used by ManagerInvitationView, OwnerAppointmentView, CompanyRegistrationView, SubmitComplaintView. A .form-narrow Div (max-width 560px) containing a series of LkCards, each a logical section of the form, with a footer row of Cancel + Submit buttons. Three lines of structural Java per section card.

Pattern 3 — Master/detail inbox

CompanyInquiryInboxView, AdminComplaintQueueView, plus SupportInboxView from 07. Three views, one shape: filter chips + md-split + MdConvRow + MdThread + MdReplyBar. The view layer is ~80 lines; the messaging components carry the weight.

Pattern 4 — Per-section + per-row capability filtering

Three levels of gating coexist:

  • View level — @RequireCapability annotation. Blocks the URL.
  • Section level — if (Capabilities.has(X)) col.add(card);. Drops a whole card.
  • Row-action level — if (Capabilities.has(X)) actions.add(icon);. Drops individual buttons.

EventManagementView uses all three: annotation for the view, section gate for the danger zone, row gate for buttons inside cards. Layered RBAC — nothing visible that the user can't use.

Pattern 5 — Stats row + filter chips + grid card

CompanySalesView, GlobalHistoryView, AdminDashboardView. A three-section shape that fits "show me KPIs at a glance, then let me drill in." Stats row at top, filter chips below, grid card at bottom. The buyer-side BrowseEventsView uses a variant (no stats row).

The view tier writes itself once you internalize the kit. Most of the 17 views in this tour are 100–200 lines of Java — the kit's composition is doing the heavy lifting. The two exceptions are PurchasePolicyEditorView (which has its own AST + JS bridge) and the messaging views (which lean on the MdThread/MdReplyBar/MdConvRow trio — still short, just behavior-rich).
